3D Printed Damselfish Model


This is a 3D printed model of the damselfish, Chromis ovalis, skull and pectoral girdle. This model was created by CT scanning the damselfish, segmenting out each bone individually from the larger scan in 3Dslicer, and then using Meshlab and Meshmixer to clean and scale up the model. The model is 20X the size of the damselfish, and is aproximately 1.5 feet from the fin radials to the tip of the premaxilla.



This image shows the opposite side of the 3D model. This side has all of the bones to the surface of the fish included, such as the opercular and occular bones, whereas on the picture above these bones have been excluded to allow a view of the more interior bones.
