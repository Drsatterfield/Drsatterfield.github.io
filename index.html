Using 3D videography to
estimate Diversity in Reef Fish Swimming Behavior

Currently I am collecting data on fish swimming behaviors from stereo video collected off the coast of Mayotte, a small island in the Mozambique Channel. My aim is to examine how diverse reef fish are in their swimming modes and identify functional traits which are related to diversity in swimming behaviors.

Local Adaptation in Behavior
in response to moderate gradients in selection

New Paper Alert: Can populations become locally adapted in behavior even with moderate spatial variation in selection pressure? We measured reactivity to risk in populations of black surfperch, Embiotoca jacksoni (a non-model marine fish) which experience different levels of spearfishing pressure. We found clear evidence of local adaptation such that high risk populations are much more reactive than low risk populations.

Effects of size and sex on
Courting success and foraging behaviour

How do body size and sex influence how fish allocate time to foraging versus reproductive activities? We measured the effects of size and sex on courting success and foraging behaviour of black surfperch Embiotoca jacksoni off Santa Catalina Island. Pairs that were closely matched in size had long courting sessions, whereas those that differed considerably in size courted only briefly. Small fish foraged more than larger fish, both during and outside the mating season. Males and females foraged at similar rates outside of the mating season, but during the mating season males reduced their foraging rates to less than half that seen outside of the mating season, whereas females continued to forage at the same rate.

Geographic variation in demography
of black perch (Embiotoca jacksoni)

Spatial variation in demography is common among populations of marine fish. However, the factors which have the greatest effect on demography and demographic rates are often unclear. We tested whether spatial variation in demography was associated with several explanatory variables including population density, predator density, food availability, and fishing activity. Spatial variation in growth was most sensitive to variation in food availability and mean age was lowest at locations with high levels of spearfishing activity.
